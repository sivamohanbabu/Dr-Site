Frequently asked questions

Vertigo is the sensation of spinning or whirling, often caused by inner ear or central nervous system issues. A Balance Disorder is a general feeling of unsteadiness. Dr. Ranjith G uses specialized clinical and Neurodiagnostic Testing to distinguish the cause, focusing on the central (brain) versus peripheral (inner ear) source.

Dr. Ranjith G employs a range of tests to accurately evaluate the cause of vertigo and balance disorders. These may include clinical balance assessments, specific maneuvers (like the Dix-Hallpike test), and advanced imaging or Neurodiagnostic Testing to check for central causes.

For common peripheral vertigo disorders like BPPV (Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo), Dr. Ranjith G often utilizes specific physical maneuvers (like the Epley maneuver) to reposition crystals in the inner ear. He also provides medication and vestibular rehabilitation therapy for chronic balance issues.

Yes, vertigo can sometimes be a sign of a central neurological problem, such as a posterior circulation stroke or a lesion in the cerebellum. Dr. Ranjith G's expertise as an Intervention Neurologist is critical in immediately recognizing and treating these potentially life-threatening causes.

For chronic balance disorders, Dr. Ranjith G creates a personalized rehabilitation plan. This includes medication management and referral to specialized physical therapy (vestibular rehabilitation) aimed at improving stability, reducing the risk of falls, and enhancing overall mobility.

Stroke is a medical emergency that occurs when blood flow to the brain is interrupted, leading to potential brain damage and long-term disability. At AB neuro centre, Dr. Ranjith G, a renowned interventional neurologist in Manikonda, provides rapid diagnosis and advanced treatment for all types of strokes, ensuring better outcomes and faster recovery.

Using cutting-edge neuroimaging, mechanical thrombectomy, and minimally invasive interventions, Dr. Ranjith delivers precise, life-saving care. His patient-centered approach addresses both acute and post-stroke rehabilitation, restoring brain function and improving quality of life for stroke survivors.

Frequently asked questions

Dr. Ranjith G is a specialist in Stroke Diagnosis & Treatment due to his DM (Neurology) and advanced Fellowship in Stroke and Intervention Neurology. This training signifies high-level expertise in the rapid, time-sensitive diagnosis of stroke and performing emergency interventional procedures.

For acute ischemic stroke (caused by a blood clot), Dr. Ranjith G provides emergency treatments, which include intravenous thrombolysis (clot-busting medication) and, when appropriate, mechanical thrombectomy (physically removing the clot). His interventional expertise is key to maximizing patient recovery.

The warning signs of a stroke are remembered by the acronym FAST: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call emergency services. Recognizing these signs and seeking immediate medical attention is vital, as timely intervention by Dr. Ranjith G can prevent permanent disability.

Yes, beyond emergency treatment, Dr. Ranjith G provides comprehensive long-term care for stroke. This includes identifying and managing risk factors (hypertension, diabetes), prescribing anti-platelet or anti-coagulation medication, and coordinating rehabilitation to ensure optimal recovery and prevention of recurrence.

Dr. Ranjith G uses advanced imaging techniques (CT scans, MRI, CT angiography) to quickly determine the type, location, and extent of the stroke. This rapid, accurate diagnosis is fundamental to his role as an Intervention Neurologist for initiating time-critical treatment.

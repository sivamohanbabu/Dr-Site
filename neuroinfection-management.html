Frequently asked questions

Neuro infections are infections that affect the brain, spinal cord, or surrounding tissues. Common examples include meningitis, encephalitis, brain abscess, and neurocysticercosis.

Symptoms may include fever, severe headache, confusion, seizures, neck stiffness, vomiting, sensitivity to light, and changes in consciousness.

Diagnosis usually involves neurological examination, brain imaging such as MRI or CT scans, blood tests, and sometimes cerebrospinal fluid analysis.

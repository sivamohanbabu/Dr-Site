Our Neurology Procedures

At AB neuro centre, Dr. Ranjith G, a leading interventional neurologist in Manikonda, Hyderabad, provides advanced neurointerventional procedures to diagnose and treat complex neurological and cerebrovascular disorders. Our state-of-the-art procedural techniques help us restore blood flow, prevent strokes, manage aneurysms, and treat vascular malformations with precision and safety.

What Are Neurointerventional Procedures?

Neurointerventional procedures are minimally invasive techniques performed using catheters and imaging guidance to treat disorders of the brain, spinal cord, and associated blood vessels. These procedures are performed under local anesthesia with sedation, ensuring minimal discomfort and faster recovery while avoiding open surgery.

Mechanical Thrombectomy

Mechanical thrombectomy is a life-saving procedure to remove blood clots from the brain in patients with acute ischemic stroke.

Conditions Treated:

  • Acute ischemic stroke
  • Large vessel occlusion
  • Brain infarction
  • Blocked cerebral arteries

Procedure Overview:

A catheter is guided through the blood vessels to the site of the clot. Using specialized devices, the clot is carefully removed to restore blood flow, minimizing brain damage and improving recovery outcomes.

Carotid Stenting

Carotid stenting is used to open narrowed carotid arteries, preventing stroke and improving cerebral blood flow.

Conditions Treated:

  • Carotid artery stenosis
  • High stroke risk
  • Transient ischemic attacks (TIA)
  • Atherosclerotic blockages

Procedure Overview:

A stent is inserted through a catheter into the carotid artery to widen the vessel. This minimally invasive procedure reduces the risk of stroke while avoiding open surgery.

Aneurysm Coiling

Aneurysm coiling treats brain aneurysms by filling the weakened vessel with coils to prevent rupture.

Conditions Treated:

  • Cerebral aneurysms
  • Risk of rupture
  • Subarachnoid hemorrhage prevention
  • Vascular malformations

Procedure Overview:

A microcatheter is inserted through the blood vessels to the aneurysm. Platinum coils are deployed inside the aneurysm, stabilizing it and preventing bleeding, without the need for open brain surgery.

AVM Embolization

AVM (Arteriovenous Malformation) embolization is a minimally invasive procedure to block abnormal blood vessels in the brain.

Conditions Treated:

  • Cerebral AVMs
  • Risk of hemorrhage
  • Seizures from AVMs
  • Vascular malformations

Procedure Overview:

A catheter delivers embolic material to the AVM, blocking abnormal blood flow. This reduces the risk of bleeding and can be combined with surgery or radiosurgery for complete treatment.

Intracranial Stenting

Intracranial stenting opens narrowed arteries within the brain to restore blood flow and prevent stroke.

Conditions Treated:

  • Intracranial artery stenosis
  • High stroke risk
  • Cerebral ischemia
  • Vessel blockages

Procedure Overview:

A stent is carefully guided to the narrowed vessel using a catheter. Once deployed, it widens the artery, ensuring adequate blood supply to the brain while minimizing complications.

Digital Subtraction Angiography (DSA)

DSA is an advanced imaging procedure to visualize blood vessels in the brain and spine, aiding precise diagnosis and intervention.

Conditions Treated:

  • Cerebral aneurysms
  • AVMs and vascular malformations
  • Stroke diagnosis
  • Spinal vascular disorders

Procedure Overview:

A catheter is inserted into the blood vessels and contrast dye is injected. X-ray images capture detailed vascular maps, guiding interventional procedures like thrombectomy, stenting, or coiling.

Spinal Angiography

Spinal angiography provides detailed images of spinal blood vessels to diagnose and treat vascular disorders.

Conditions Treated:

  • Spinal AVMs
  • Tumor vascular supply
  • Vascular malformations
  • Spinal hemorrhage risk

Procedure Overview:

A catheter is inserted into the spinal arteries, and contrast dye is injected. High-resolution images identify abnormalities, allowing Dr. Ranjith to plan minimally invasive procedures accurately.

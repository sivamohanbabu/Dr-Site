Frequently asked questions

In the initial stage of a Head Injury (Traumatic Brain Injury), Dr. Ranjith G's role is critical. He performs a thorough neurological assessment, orders appropriate imaging (CT/MRI), manages acute complications (like seizures or bleeding), and determines the need for neurosurgical consultation.

When suspecting a Brain Tumor, Dr. Ranjith G uses a comprehensive evaluation including a neurological exam and advanced imaging (MRI with contrast). This helps localize the tumor, assess its impact on brain function, and guide the next steps, such as biopsy or surgical planning.

Yes, Dr. Ranjith G manages the long-term effects of a Head Injury, which can include post-concussion syndrome, headaches, cognitive deficits, and post-traumatic epilepsy. His management plan focuses on rehabilitation and symptom control.

For Brain Tumors, Dr. Ranjith G coordinates a multidisciplinary team approach. He collaborates closely with neurosurgeons, oncologists, and radiation specialists to formulate a comprehensive treatment plan that may involve surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation therapy.

Dr. Ranjith G is committed to post-treatment recovery. He evaluates cognitive functions and coordinates with neuropsychologists and rehabilitation specialists to address any deficits resulting from the Head Injury or Brain Tumor, optimizing the patient's return to independence.
